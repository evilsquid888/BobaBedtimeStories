# Story 24: Mochi and the Demon Slumber Party

**Main Character:** Mochi (麻糬 máshǔ) — Mochi
**Guest Characters:** None
**Setting:** Wulai Hot Springs, New Taipei City
**Theme:** Love stretches to fit everyone
**Mandarin Spotlight:** 麻糬冰淇淋 (máshǔ bīngqílín) = mochi ice cream

---

The steam rising from the **烏來** (Wūlái) = Wulai hot springs smelled like warm rocks and sleepy mountains, and Mochi was having the time of her soft, pillowy life.

She was sitting on the edge of a stone pool, her round white body dusted in potato starch, her rosy pink cheeks practically glowing in the moonlight, and her stubby little arms stretched out in a pose that she called "Maximum Relaxation Mode." Her feet dangled in the hot water. Tiny bubbles tickled her toes. Somewhere in the cedar trees, a night bird was singing something that sounded suspiciously like a lullaby.

"This," Mochi announced to absolutely nobody, "is the COZIEST night in the HISTORY of nights!"

And then twelve demon children crashed out of the forest.

Now, before you get worried — and I can see your eyebrows going up from here — these were not scary demons. These were *tiny* demons. We're talking knee-high, round-bellied, stubby-horned little **小鬼** (xiǎoguǐ) = little demons, and every single one of them was either crying, shivering, or doing both at the same time.

The first one stumbled into the clearing and bonked right into a lantern post. The second tripped over the first. The third through twelfth arrived in a rolling, tumbling, wailing pile of miniature catastrophe, like someone had knocked over a barrel of very upset kittens — except these kittens had tiny horns and their tears made a sizzling sound when they hit the ground, leaving little scorch marks on the stone path.

(Adorable? Absolutely. A minor fire hazard? Also yes.)

"We're LOST!" howled the smallest demon, who was blue with orange horns and approximately the size of a cantaloupe. "We were having a slumber party in the spirit world and Pao-Pao said he knew a shortcut and NOW WE'RE IN THE HUMAN WORLD AND IT'S COLD AND I WANT MY BLANKIE!"

Mochi's eyes went wide.

Her cheeks flushed an even deeper pink.

Her arms — those wonderfully stretchy, taffy-like arms — began to extend.

"Oh no," she whispered.

Then louder: "Oh NO."

Then at full volume, with the force of a thousand maternal instincts detonating at once: "COME HERE RIGHT NOW AND LET ME HUG YOU!!!"

She launched herself off the edge of the pool and stretched. And stretched. And *stretched.* Her arms extended outward like two great ribbons of warm, pillowy dough, swooping left to scoop up three demon kids, arcing right to gather four more, then looping around in an enormous circle to collect the remaining five in one spectacular, physics-defying embrace.

Twelve demon children. One Mochi. The world's most aggressive group hug.

"You are FREEZING!" Mochi cried, squishing them all against her soft, warm body. "You are TINY! You are PRECIOUS! Nobody panic — Auntie Mochi is HERE!"

"You're really squishy," observed a green demon kid, face-first in Mochi's side.

"THANK YOU! That is the NICEST thing anyone has ever said to me! Now — everyone into the hot springs. Immediately. No arguments. We are WARMING UP."

She stretched herself into a kind of living conveyor belt and gently deposited all twelve demon children into the nearest pool.

What happened next was, well — have you ever seen twelve tiny demons experience a hot spring for the very first time?

The blue cantaloupe demon sank in up to his horns and made a sound like a tea kettle releasing pressure. "Ahhhhhhh."

A red demon with three eyes sniffed the water suspiciously. "Is this soup?"

"It's NOT soup," Mochi said.

A purple demon took a big slurp. "Tastes like soup."

"Please do NOT drink the hot spring —"

A yellow demon the shape of a dumpling climbed onto a rock, yelled "WATCH THIS!" and executed a cannonball so spectacular that it sent a wave of water crashing over everyone, including Mochi, including a very startled night bird in a nearby tree, and including a small stone statue of a frog that, for the record, had been having a perfectly peaceful evening until that moment.

"AGAIN! AGAIN!" screamed all twelve demons.

The dumpling demon obliged. Four more cannonballs. Each one bigger. Each one splashier. By the third one, Mochi had stretched herself into an umbrella shape over the littlest demons to keep them from being washed away entirely.

"Okay!" Mochi laughed, reforming herself into her usual blob shape and shaking water off her starch-dusted surface. "Is everyone warm? Are all horns accounted for? Let me count — one, two, three... seven... eleven... twelve! Perfect! Nobody floated away!"

For a few minutes, everything was peaceful. Steam curled up from the water. The demons soaked and sighed and splashed gently. The mountains around Wulai stood dark and quiet against a sky full of stars. Mochi sat at the edge of the pool with her stretchy arms draped around as many demon kids as she could reach, which was all of them, because Mochi's arms could reach approximately anything if love was involved.

And then a tiny voice piped up.

"Auntie Mochi? I'm hungry."

Eleven other tiny voices immediately agreed.

"I want a snack!"

"Me too!"

"I want **麻糬冰淇淋** (máshǔ bīngqílín) = mochi ice cream! That's what we were gonna have at our slumber party!"

"MOCHI ICE CREAM! MOCHI ICE CREAM!"

The chant started small and grew until all twelve demon children were bouncing in the hot spring, sending waves sloshing over the edges, their little horns bobbing, their voices rising into the misty mountain night: "MO-CHI ICE CREAM! MO-CHI ICE CREAM!"

Mochi's heart swelled. Mochi ice cream! Their favorite! The spirit world's number one slumber party snack! Of course they wanted mochi ice cream — it was perfect and delicious and wrapped in soft, chewy —

She paused.

She looked down at herself.

Soft.

Chewy.

Stretchy.

She was literally made of mochi.

"Oh," she said softly, a grin spreading across her rosy face. "Oh oh oh."

She bounced to her feet. "NOBODY MOVE! Auntie Mochi has a PLAN and it is the BEST plan and it involves a GROUP HUG with ICE CREAM!"

She stretched one arm approximately three blocks to the nearest **便利商店** (biànlì shāngdiàn) = convenience store — because in Taiwan, there is always a convenience store nearby, even in a mountain hot spring town at midnight, because Taiwan is a magical place — and snagged twelve individual cups of ice cream. Vanilla, strawberry, mango, taro, red bean, and one mystery flavor that turned out to be honeydew melon, which made the three-eyed red demon so happy he actually floated six inches off the ground.

Then Mochi took a deep breath.

"Okay, babies. Watch Auntie Mochi work."

She stretched herself thin. Really thin. Pizza-dough thin. Thinner than she'd ever stretched before — a wide, translucent sheet of beautiful, pillowy mochi dough, dusted with starch, glowing faintly in the moonlight like a soft white blanket made of pure love and glutinous rice flour.

And then she wrapped.

She wrapped herself around the first scoop of ice cream — a perfect little sphere of strawberry — and pinched the edges closed with a satisfying *smoosh.* One down. She tore off that piece and started on the next. Vanilla. *Smoosh.* Mango. *Smoosh.* Taro, red bean, another vanilla, two more strawberries, mango again, mystery honeydew, and one final red bean.

Twelve perfect balls of mochi ice cream, each one wrapped in a piece of Mochi herself, each one a tiny edible hug.

She was thinner now, sure. A little see-through in places. Definitely more stretched out than usual. But her grin? Her grin was enormous.

"COME AND GET IT!" she sang.

The demon children descended on the mochi ice cream like a very small, very adorable swarm of hornets. They grabbed and bit and chewed and moaned with happiness. Ice cream dripped down tiny demon chins. Cheeks bulged. The cannonball dumpling demon shoved an entire mochi ice cream ball in his mouth at once and had to sit very still for thirty seconds with his cheeks puffed out like a chipmunk while his friends pounded him on the back.

"Ish sho ghood," he managed, spraying crumbs.

The blue cantaloupe demon ate his slowly, savoring every bite, tears rolling down his face — happy tears this time, though they still sizzled when they hit the ground.

"This tastes like the ones from home," he whispered.

Mochi — stretched thin, slightly translucent, and happier than she had ever been in her entire squishy existence — gathered up every last demon child in her remaining arms and squeezed.

"That's because love tastes the same EVERYWHERE," she said, her voice wobbling just a tiny bit. "Now finish your ice cream, you wonderful little monsters. It's bedtime."

One by one, the demon children's eyes grew heavy. Full bellies. Warm water. The gentle steam of the hot springs. Mochi stretched herself into a kind of living blanket — because of course she did — and draped herself over the whole pile of drowsy demons as they curled up on the warm stones beside the pool.

Twelve tiny demons in a warm, squishy heap, wrapped in Mochi, wrapped in steam, wrapped in the quiet of the Wulai mountains at the deepest, softest hour of the night.

The dumpling demon yawned so wide his horns wiggled. "Auntie Mochi?"

"Yes, baby?"

"You're the best blanket ever."

"I am CRYING," Mochi whispered. "These are HAPPY TEARS. Nobody look at me."

The last demon kid fell asleep with a tiny snore that sounded like a kitten purring through a kazoo.

And Mochi?

Mochi lay there, thin as a crepe, stretched across twelve sleeping demon babies, staring up at the stars through the hot spring mist, and thought to herself:

*I got to HUG ice cream AND demon babies in the SAME NIGHT.*

*BEST. NIGHT. EVER!!!*

Somewhere around three in the morning, a shimmering door opened in the air above the hot springs, and a very large, very worried-looking demon parent poked their head through, followed by eleven more very large, very worried-looking demon parents.

They found their children sleeping peacefully in a warm pile, guarded by a slightly translucent, extremely contented blob of mochi who whisper-shouted "SHHHHH! They JUST fell asleep! Take them GENTLY! And COME BACK ANYTIME — Auntie Mochi gives FREE HUGS and SNACKS!"

The demon parents bowed deeply, scooped up their snoring children, and disappeared back into the spirit world with twelve sleeping demon kids and a story they would tell for generations.

Mochi, alone again by the hot springs, slowly puffed herself back to her full, round, pillowy shape. She dusted herself with fresh potato starch, settled back into the warm water, and sighed the most satisfied sigh that has ever been sighed.

"Love stretches to fit everyone," she murmured. Then she giggled. "Get it? Because I LITERALLY stretched? I'm hilarious. Someone should write that down."

(Someone did.)

## Goodnight Blessing

May you always stretch a little further than you think you can — for a hug, for a friend, for a tiny lost someone who needs you. May your heart be as warm as the Wulai hot springs and as soft as Mochi's arms. May you fall asleep knowing that you are wrapped in love, and that love never runs out, no matter how thin you spread it. It just gets bigger. Goodnight, little one. Auntie Mochi would absolutely hug you right now if she could. And honestly? She's probably trying. Sweet dreams.
