# Storyboard: Story 25 — Tāng Yuán and the Demon King's Midnight Snack

**Platform:** CapCut Video Studio + Seedance 2.0
**Total Shots:** 13
**Estimated Runtime:** ~3:15-3:45
**Art Style (use on ALL shots):** Whimsical storybook illustration, ancient Taiwanese town atmosphere, warm amber and gold against deep teal/blue night, fog and lantern light

> **Tip:** Generate reference images of Tāng Yuán (perfectly round white rice ball, rosy cheeks, visible black sesame heart, personal steam aura) and the Demon King (thirty feet tall, green scales, curling ram horns, yellow eyes, surprisingly gentle) in Shots 1-3. Attach to all subsequent shots.

---

## Shot 1 — BOUNCING THROUGH LUKANG (0:00–0:15)

**Visual Prompt:**
`A perfectly round white glutinous rice ball with rosy cheeks bouncing joyfully down ancient cobblestone streets at night. Narrow alleys, crumbling brick temples, old shophouses, red lanterns swinging in wind, fog creeping between buildings. Faint steam rises around the rice ball like a personal hot spring. Whimsical storybook illustration, warm amber tones against deep blue night.`

**Narration:**
> "The coldest night of the year had arrived in Lukang, and Tāng Yuán was having the time of his life. He bounced down the ancient cobblestone streets like a ping-pong ball on a trampoline. 'What a BEAUTIFUL night to be round!'"

## Shot 2 — THE EARTH SHAKES (0:15–0:30)

**Visual Prompt:**
`An ancient stone temple courtyard at night with cobblestones cracking and splitting, a zigzag crack running across the ground. A tiny white rice ball stands frozen mid-bounce. Red lanterns swing wildly, roof tiles clatter. A stray cat launches straight up in terror. Dramatic, ominous but still whimsical storybook illustration, deep blue and amber tones.`

**Narration:**
> "The ground shook. Not a little shake — a the-entire-earth-just-hiccupped shake. The cobblestones rattled. A crack split the stone courtyard like dark lightning. And from that crack, something began to rise."

## Shot 3 — THE DEMON KING RISES (0:30–0:50)

**Visual Prompt:**
`An enormous thirty-foot demon with dark green scales, massive curling ram horns, and glowing yellow eyes rising from the cracked earth of an ancient temple courtyard. He towers over temple rooftops. But he's sneezing — a massive explosive sneeze — looking absolutely miserable with watery eyes and shivering scales. A tiny white rice ball tumbles backward from the blast. Epic yet comedic, dramatic storybook illustration.`

**Narration:**
> "First came the horns — each the size of a motorcycle. Then a head the size of a minivan. Thirty feet of scales, horns, and claws. He opened his terrifying jaws and let out a — actually, no. He let out a sneeze. 'AAAA-CHOOOO!'"

## Shot 4 — "BLESS YOU, BIG GUY" (0:50–1:05)

The demon tries to look menacing but is clearly shivering. Massive size contrast between the two characters. Comedic, warm storybook illustration, temple courtyard setting.`

**Narration:**

## Shot 5 — "I WANT TANGYUAN" (1:05–1:20)

**Visual Prompt:**
`Close-up of the Demon King's massive face — green scales, glowing yellow eyes gone soft and faraway, lower lip (the size of a canoe) trembling. A tiny embarrassed expression on a thirty-foot monster. Fog and lantern light. Emotional, intimate close-up despite the enormous scale. Warm storybook illustration.`

**Narration:**

## Shot 6 — "A THOUSAND YEARS?!" (1:20–1:35)

**Visual Prompt:**
`A tiny white rice ball with an expression of absolute horror, arms raised in shock. A massive demon king sits hunched and sad behind him. Temple courtyard, fog, red lanterns. The rice ball is dramatically backlit. Comedic overreaction, warm storybook illustration.`

**Narration:**
> "'A THOUSAND YEARS without tangyuan?' Tāng Yuán was horrified. Truly horrified. This was the worst thing he had ever heard. 'That's a tragedy, family. An ACTUAL tragedy. But we are going to FIX this. Tonight. Right now.'"

## Production Notes

- **Character consistency:** Tāng Yuán = perfectly round white rice ball, rosy cheeks, faint steam aura. Demon King = thirty feet, green scales, curling ram horns, yellow eyes, surprisingly gentle.
- **Scale contrast:** The comedy and heart come from the massive size difference. Emphasize it in every shared shot.
- **Energy arc:** Playful → dramatic rise → comedic sneeze → emotional backstory → cooking montage → triumphant cannonball → tearful tasting → joyful community → peaceful sleep.
- **Audio:** Tāng Yuán = hearty, warm, inclusive voice. Demon King = deep rumble that softens to a whisper. Consider erhu music for the closing scenes.
- **Music:** Playful bounce for opening, dramatic rumble for the rise, tender piano for the tear scene, warm folk music with erhu for the community feast, soft lullaby for closing.
- **Transitions:** Hard cut for the earthquake, slow dissolve for emotional beats, gentle fade to black on the final shot.
