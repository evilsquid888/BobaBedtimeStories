# Storyboard: Story 13 — Passion Fruit and the Sour Dare

**Platform:** CapCut Video Studio + Seedance 2.0
**Total Shots:** 12
**Estimated Runtime:** ~3:00-3:30
**Art Style (use on ALL shots):** Whimsical storybook illustration, Taitung wild palette — purple-brown shells, vibrant orange pulp, deep tropical green, hot spring steam-mist, deep blue river — soft edges, bold adventurous warmth

> **Tip:** Generate a reference image of Bǎi (round wrinkly purple-brown passion fruit, mischievous grin, one section cracked open showing bright orange pulp with crunchy black seeds) and Jiàn-Háo (13-year-old quiet boy, helmet, school adventure clothes) in Shots 1 and 5. Attach to all subsequent shots.

---

## Shot 1 — PUCKER UP, BUTTERCUP (0:00–0:15)

**Visual Prompt:**
`A round wrinkly purple-brown passion fruit with a mischievous grin and one section cracked open revealing bright orange pulp with crunchy black seeds, squirting a stream of tangy orange juice at a passing butterfly that dodges. Sun-warmed rock in tropical Taitung valley. Cheeky, energetic storybook illustration with purple, orange, and gold tones.`

**Narration:**
> "Bǎi was trouble, and she loved every single second of it. 'Pucker up, buttercup!' she cackled, squirting a stream of tangy orange juice at a passing butterfly. The butterfly dodged. Bǎi did not care. She was already looking for her next target. She was surprise in fruit form. She was a dare with a stem."

## Shot 3 — DARING THE TANGERINE (0:30–0:45)

**Visual Prompt:**
`A wrinkly purple passion fruit gleefully daring a nervous orange tangerine to jump into a steaming sulfur pool. The tangerine looks horrified. Volcanic stone basins, steam rising. Comedic, mischievous storybook illustration with orange and steam-mist tones.`

**Narration:**
> "'Dare you to jump in the sulfur pool!' she shouted at a nervous tangerine. 'No way! It smells like rotten eggs!' 'That's the sulfur, baby! It's GOOD for you!' The tangerine fled. Bǎi shrugged. Not everyone was built for adventure. 'Life is too short to be sweet all the time! Sometimes you gotta be SOUR!'"

## Shot 4 — JIÀN-HÁO IN THE BACK (0:45–1:05)

**Visual Prompt:**
`A 13-year-old quiet boy in adventure clothes and a helmet standing at the back of a noisy school group at the river entrance. The instructor with a megaphone explains river-tracing safety. **Narration:**
> "Jiàn-Háo was thirteen, visiting Taitung with his school group. The kind of kid who stood at the back of every line, spoke quietly, and had perfected the art of being invisible. The activity was river tracing — hiking upstream, climbing boulders, navigating waterfalls. 'I don't think I can do this,' he whispered."

She bounced down, rolled along the riverbank, and popped up on a boulder right in his path. 'Hey, kid! You look like you're having the time of your life,' her voice dripping with cheerful sarcasm."

## Shot 6 — SOUR THEN SWEET LECTURE (1:20–1:45)

**Visual Prompt:**
`A wrinkly purple passion fruit speaking earnestly to a quiet boy on a sunny rock by a mountain river. Behind them, a small waterfall where other kids are climbing. The passion fruit's bright orange pulp visibly swirls inside her cracked shell as she gestures. Animated, philosophical storybook illustration with sunny green and orange.`

**Narration:**
> "'You know what happens when someone cuts me open? First they SEE me — weird orange seedy goop. Not Instagram-worthy. ALARMING. Then they TASTE me — full-face, eye-squinting SOUR. And THEN the sweet hits. AFTER the sour. BECAUSE of the sour. The sweetness only works because the sourness came first. The thing you're scared of — that's the sour part.'"

## Shot 8 — CLIMBING THE WATERFALL (2:00–2:25)

**Visual Prompt:**
`A boy in a helmet climbing a small two-meter waterfall in a clear mountain river, water cascading around him, his foot slipping but catching, his hand slipping but catching, water pouring over his helmet. **Narration:**
> "He climbed. His foot slipped once. He caught himself. His hand slipped once. He caught himself again. Water poured over his helmet and into his eyes. He couldn't see but he could FEEL — the rock under his fingers, the current pulling at his legs, the pure, electric, terrifying, SOUR thrill of doing something he'd been too scared to do."

Then he pumped both fists in the air and YELLED. Just sound. Raw, joyful, completely unselfconscious sound. The kind that comes from the place where fear turns into freedom. 'Tài bàng le!' his classmates shouted."

## Production Notes

- **Character consistency:** Bǎi = round wrinkly purple-brown shell, mischievous grin, ALWAYS one section cracked open showing bright orange pulp and crunchy black seeds. Often spraying juice. Jiàn-Háo = 13-year-old quiet boy, helmet for river-tracing scenes, transformation arc from invisible to triumphant.
- **Art style consistency:** Include "whimsical storybook illustration, Taitung wild palette of purple, orange, and tropical green" in every prompt. Settings: hot springs steam (1-3, 11-12), mountain river (4-9), valley vista (10).
- **Audio:** Bǎi's voice cheeky, daring, full of cackles. Jiàn-Háo's voice quiet and uncertain at first, mostly silent during the climb (just breathing and grunting), then RAW JOY at Shot 9 — mostly wordless yell.
- **Music:** Mischievous bouncy strings for Bǎi. Building tension for the river-tracing scenes. Massive triumphant percussion at Shot 9 (the yell). Soft warm strings for the hot spring scene. Lullaby for goodnight.
- **Transitions:** Quick playful cuts during Bǎi's pranks. Slow building transitions during the climb (Shot 8). Hold long on Shot 9 (the yell of triumph) and Shot 11 (the sour-to-sweet face).
- **Each clip:** 5–15 seconds via Seedance 2.0. Shot 8 (climbing) and Shot 9 (top of waterfall yell) deserve longer holds — these are the emotional climax.
