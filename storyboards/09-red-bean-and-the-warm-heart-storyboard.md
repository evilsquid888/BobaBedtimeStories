# Storyboard: Story 09 — Red Bean and the Warm Heart

**Platform:** CapCut Video Studio + Seedance 2.0
**Total Shots:** 12
**Estimated Runtime:** ~3:00-3:30
**Art Style (use on ALL shots):** Whimsical storybook illustration, deep burgundy and warm grandmother-kitchen palette — burgundy red, golden brown, steam-white, soft amber — soft edges, nurturing warmth

> **Tip:** Generate a reference image of Hóng Dòu (small plump deep burgundy red bean with a white stripe down one side, tiny ladle, warm grandmotherly expression) and Grandma Liú (elderly Taiwanese woman, kind crinkly eyes, apron) in Shot 1. Attach to all subsequent shots.

---

## Shot 1 — STIRRING THE POT (0:00–0:15)

**Visual Prompt:**
`A tiny deep burgundy red bean with a white stripe stirring a giant iron cauldron of red bean soup with a ladle three times her size, in a small steamy traditional Taiwanese douhua shop kitchen. Warm grandmotherly expression. Steam rises in soft white wisps. Cozy storybook illustration with burgundy, gold, and steam-white tones.`

**Narration:**
> "Hóng Dòu was a hóngdòu — a single, plump, deep burgundy bean with a tiny white stripe and a warm grandmotherly expression. 'Everyone needs a little sweetness,' she said, stirring the pot with a ladle three times her size. 'That's what red bean is for. We're the warm thing at the bottom of the bowl that makes you close your eyes and think of home.'"

---

## Shot 2 — GRANDMA LIÚ'S SHOP (0:15–0:30)

**Visual Prompt:**
`A tiny douhua shop on Yǒngkāng Street in Taipei — six tables, a counter, a line of customers stretching down the block. Warm interior light, steam rising from bowls. Cozy storybook illustration with deep red, golden brown, and warm window light.`

**Narration:**
> "Grandma Liú's shop was legendary. It had been on Yǒngkāng Street for forty-seven years, serving dòuhuā with toppings: red bean, peanuts, taro balls, and sweet ginger syrup. The shop was tiny — just six tables — but the line outside stretched down the block every afternoon."

---

## Shot 3 — GRANDMA TIRED (0:30–0:50)

**Visual Prompt:**
`An elderly Taiwanese woman with kind crinkled eyes — Grandma Liú — moving slowly behind her shop counter, her hands trembling slightly as she lifts a pot lid. No song this morning, just silence. A tiny red bean watches with worry from inside the simmering pot. Tender, slightly somber storybook illustration with warm shop light.`

**Narration:**
> "On a Thursday, Grandma Liú came in looking different. Tired. Her hands shook when she lifted the pot lids. She didn't sing while she cooked, which was unprecedented. Hóng Dòu noticed immediately. 'Something's wrong. Look at her eyes.'"

---

## Shot 4 — THE LETTER (0:50–1:10)

**Visual Prompt:**
`Grandma Liú sitting at one of her own shop tables, head in her hands, a folded letter from a hospital between her fingers. A tiny red bean has climbed across the counter and sits on the table in front of the letter, leaving tiny burgundy footprints on the white tablecloth. Heartbreaking, intimate storybook illustration with soft afternoon light.`

**Narration:**
> "At noon, Grandma Liú sat down at one of her own tables — something she never did — and pulled a letter from her apron pocket. From a hospital. Hóng Dòu climbed out of the pot, made her way across the counter, and onto the table. She was dripping soup. She didn't care. 'Oh,' said Hóng Dòu. Just 'oh.'"

---

## Shot 5 — A TINY BEAN OFFERS (1:10–1:30)

**Visual Prompt:**
`Grandma Liú holding a tiny burgundy red bean gently between two fingers up to eye level, both surprised and tender. The little bean's expression resolute and caring. Soft golden afternoon light through the shop window. Warm, intimate storybook illustration with deep red and golden tones.`

**Narration:**
> "'How did you get out here? Are you worried about me, little bean?' Hóng Dòu nodded. 'Go home. Rest. I'll take care of the shop.' 'A bean can't run a shop.' 'A bean who has watched you cook for forty-seven years knows every recipe by heart. Low heat. Slow time. A pinch of sugar. I've got this.'"

---

## Shot 6 — KITCHEN COMMAND (1:30–1:50)

**Visual Prompt:**
`A small douhua shop kitchen with a tiny burgundy red bean standing on top of a sugar jar issuing orders. Round taro balls roll along the counter to turn gas knobs. Teams of peanuts work together stirring pots. A dark grass jelly cube in a beanie keeps the area cool. Joyful organized chaos storybook illustration with warm kitchen light.`

**Narration:**
> "Hóng Dòu took charge. She rallied the other ingredients. The taro balls turned the gas knobs. The peanuts, in teams of twelve, stirred the pots. The grass jelly (a visiting Caǎo) kept everything cool. 'Low heat!' Hóng Dòu called from her command post. 'Slow time! Don't rush the beans!' 'Jiāyóu!' the peanuts chanted."

---

## Shot 7 — THE FIRED BUSINESSMAN (1:50–2:10)

**Visual Prompt:**
`A tired businessman in a wrinkled suit and a crooked tie sitting alone at a small douhua shop table, taking his first bite of warm douhua with red beans on top. His eyes close. His shoulders drop. The clenched jaw finally relaxes. Tender storybook illustration with warm afternoon shop light.`

**Narration:**
> "The first customer was a businessman who had just been fired. Hóng Dòu made his douhua herself. The silky tofu pudding, the warm ginger syrup, a generous helping of red beans. He took one bite. His eyes closed. His shoulders dropped. 'This tastes like my grandmother's cooking,' he whispered."

---

## Shot 8 — A SHOP FULL OF SOFTENED HEARTS (2:10–2:30)

**Visual Prompt:**
`A bustling little douhua shop with a variety of customers all eating bowls of red bean douhua: a college student, a mother with a sleeping baby, an old man eating alone, a Japanese tourist, two kids who stopped fighting and are sharing a bowl. Each face soft, warm, healed. Cozy ensemble storybook illustration with deep burgundy and warm gold.`

**Narration:**
> "Then a college student. Then a mother with a crying baby. Then an old man eating alone. Then a tourist from Japan who was homesick. Then two kids who had been fighting. Hóng Dòu made every bowl with the same care. By closing time, every customer had left looking softer. Like they'd been hugged from the inside."

---

## Shot 9 — GRANDMA LIÚ RETURNS (2:30–2:50)

**Visual Prompt:**
`The next morning, Grandma Liú walking into her shop, looking rested for the first time in months, finding the place clean and a pot of red beans already simmering. She peers into the pot. Hóng Dòu floats on top, tiny ladle in hand, exhausted but triumphant. Heartwarming storybook illustration with warm morning light.`

**Narration:**
> "Grandma Liú came back the next morning. She looked rested — actually rested, for the first time in months. She peered into the pot. Hóng Dòu was floating on top, tiny ladle in hand, exhausted but triumphant. 'How was it?' 'Everyone got fed. That's what matters.'"

---

## Shot 10 — THE SECRET INGREDIENT (2:50–3:05)

**Visual Prompt:**
`Grandma Liú gently scooping a tiny red bean out of the pot and placing her on the counter beside a fresh bowl of douhua. The morning light makes the bean glow ruby-red. Tender, intimate storybook illustration with golden morning light and deep red tones.`

**Narration:**
> "'People always ask what makes my red beans special. I tell them it's the recipe. The low heat. The slow time. But it's not really.' 'What is it?' 'It's the bean that stays up all night making sure everyone else gets fed before she feeds herself.' Hóng Dòu blushed approximately the color of a ruby."

---

## Shot 11 — LET SOMEONE FEED YOU (3:05–3:20)

**Visual Prompt:**
`A tiny burgundy red bean sitting at a small shop table in front of a tiny bowl of warm red bean soup, held in place by Grandma Liú's loving wrinkled hands. Both smiling. Steam rising from the tiny bowl. Soft, deeply warm storybook illustration with golden morning light and burgundy tones.`

**Narration:**
> "'Chī bǎo le ma?' Grandma Liú asked her. 'No,' Hóng Dòu admitted. 'I was so busy cooking I forgot.' Grandma Liú laughed, made a tiny bowl of red bean soup, and set it in front of the world's smallest, warmest, most nurturing bean. 'Eat. Let someone take care of YOU for once.' It was, she had to admit, hǎochī."

---

## Shot 12 — GOODNIGHT (3:20–3:35)

**Visual Prompt:**
`The little douhua shop at peaceful late evening with the lights dimmed, the iron cauldron of red bean soup simmering gently on the back burner, steam rising softly. A tiny bean sleeping curled up on the counter. Sleepy, deeply warm storybook illustration with soft amber lamplight.`

**Narration:**
> "Tonight, caring one, may you rest the way Hóng Dòu finally rested — deeply, warmly, knowing that you've done enough. You don't have to feed the whole world before you feed yourself. Tomorrow there will be a warm bowl waiting for you. Goodnight, little red bean. Goodnight."

## Production Notes

- **Character consistency:** Hóng Dòu = small plump deep burgundy bean with a white stripe, tiny ladle, grandmotherly warmth. Grandma Liú = elderly Taiwanese woman, kind crinkly eyes, apron, slow careful hands. Other ingredients (taro balls, peanuts, Caǎo) make a brief ensemble appearance in Shot 6.
- **Art style consistency:** Include "whimsical storybook illustration, deep burgundy and warm grandmother-kitchen palette" in every prompt. Steam should be visible in the kitchen scenes; sunlight slanting through the shop window in Shots 4-5, 9-11.
- **Audio:** Hóng Dòu's voice gentle, caring, grandmotherly. Grandma Liú's voice soft and tired in Shot 3, warm and grateful by Shots 9-11. Narrator extra warm.
- **Music:** Soft Taiwanese folk strings throughout. Quiet piano during the letter moment (Shot 4). Building warmth across Shots 7-8 (customers being healed). Tender lullaby for the goodnight.
- **Transitions:** Slow ceremonial dissolves throughout. Hold long on Shot 5 (the tiny bean offering) and Shot 11 (being fed).
- **Each clip:** 5–15 seconds via Seedance 2.0. Shot 4 (the letter) and Shot 7 (businessman softening) deserve longer holds for emotional weight.
