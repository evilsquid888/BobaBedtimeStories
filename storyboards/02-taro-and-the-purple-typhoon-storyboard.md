# Storyboard: Story 02 — Taro and the Purple Typhoon

**Platform:** CapCut Video Studio + Seedance 2.0
**Total Shots:** 12
**Estimated Runtime:** ~3:00-3:30
**Art Style (use on ALL shots):** Whimsical storybook illustration, lavender-and-stormy-violet palette with warm gold accents, mountain village atmosphere, soft edges, dramatic-but-cozy lighting

> **Tip:** Generate a reference image of Taro (chunky dusty purple oval, lavender-white swirls, tiny round reading glasses, sturdy stubby legs, calm wise expression) in Shot 1. Attach to all subsequent shots — his glasses are lost during the storm (Shot 6 onward).

---

## Shot 1 — JIUFEN ON THE SHELF (0:00–0:15)

**Visual Prompt:**
`A chunky purple taro chunk wearing tiny round reading glasses sitting peacefully on a wooden shelf inside a cozy Jiufen tea house, reading a small book. Red lanterns visible through the window, mountainside town in the distance. Warm, intimate storybook illustration with purple, gold, and brown tones.`

**Narration:**
> "Taro had been sitting on the same shelf in the same dessert shop on the same winding street in Jiufen for exactly three weeks, and he didn't mind one bit. 'Good things take time to steam,' he said, turning the page of a very small book about root vegetables."

---

## Shot 2 — JIUFEN OLD STREET (0:15–0:30)

**Visual Prompt:**
`Wide establishing shot of Jiufen Old Street — narrow stone steps zigzagging up a mountainside, red lanterns strung between tea houses stacked like a Jenga tower, ocean glittering in the distance. Tourists climb the steps. Magical, atmospheric storybook illustration with crimson, gold, and misty mountain blues.`

**Narration:**
> "Jiufen was the kind of place that made you believe in magic. Perched on a mountainside overlooking the ocean, the old mining town was a maze of narrow stone steps, red lanterns, and tea houses stacked on top of each other like a delicious game of Jenga."

---

## Shot 3 — GRANDMA LIN'S SHOP (0:30–0:45)

**Visual Prompt:**
`The interior of an old Taiwanese dessert shop — Grandma Lin's Taro Ball Parlor — with an ancient woman with kind crinkled eyes rolling purple taro dough into perfect spheres at a wooden counter. A purple taro chunk in reading glasses watches with admiration. Warm shop interior, storybook illustration with purple and amber tones.`

**Narration:**
> "Grandma Lin was nearly as old as the mountain. She had been making taro balls since she was twelve. 'Her secret,' Taro told visitors, 'is patience. She kneads the dough until it's perfect. She rolls each ball with love. You cannot rush love.'"

---

## Shot 4 — THE PURPLE STORM ARRIVES (0:45–1:05)

**Visual Prompt:**
`A swirling violet-tinged storm gathering above Jiufen — the sky bruised purple, the ocean churning gray, red lanterns whipping wildly on their strings. Grandma Lin pulling lanterns inside through a doorway. Atmospheric, ominous storybook illustration with purple, gold, and storm-gray.`

**Narration:**
> "The weather reports called it Typhoon Zǐsè — The Purple Typhoon — because of the strange violet tinge to its clouds. Meteorologists were baffled. And Taro felt a strange tingling in his starchy core, as if the storm were calling specifically to him."

---

## Shot 5 — THE CRY FOR HELP (1:05–1:20)

**Visual Prompt:**
`Inside the dessert shop, a purple taro chunk in reading glasses stands at the door listening intently while a pile of nervous sweet potato balls and tapioca pearls huddle together. Through the window, sheets of purple rain. Tense storybook illustration with warm interior light against stormy violet outside.`

**Narration:**
> "Through the noise, Taro heard it. A voice. Small, scared, coming from somewhere up the mountain. 'Help! Please! I'm stuck!' 'Are you INSANE?' shrieked the tapioca pearls. 'Someone needs help,' said Taro simply. 'I'll get there when I get there. Good things take time.'"

---

## Shot 6 — INTO THE STORM (1:20–1:40)

**Visual Prompt:**
`A chunky purple taro chunk leaning into a roaring purple-tinged storm, climbing the rain-slicked stone stairs of Jiufen one at a time. His tiny reading glasses swept away by the wind. Water cascades down the stairs like waterfalls. Red lanterns whip overhead. Dramatic, heroic storybook illustration with purple and stormy gold.`

**Narration:**
> "The wind hit him like a freight train made of rain. But Taro was heavy. Dense. Solid. He planted his stubby legs and leaned in. His reading glasses were swept away — 'Those were prescription' — but he pressed on. Step. By. Step."

---

## Shot 7 — THE TINY TARO BALL (1:40–2:00)

**Visual Prompt:**
`At the entrance of an old hilltop temple with its roof partly torn off, a barely-an-inch-wide pale purple baby taro ball is pinned beneath a fallen wooden beam, wide terrified eyes brimming with tears. A larger chunky purple taro arrives, calm and reassuring. Wind howls, rain pours sideways. Heart-tugging storybook illustration with stormy violet and warm amber.`

**Narration:**
> "There, trapped under a fallen beam at the temple entrance, was the smallest, most frightened taro ball Taro had ever seen. 'I — I was trying to see the typhoon,' she sniffled. 'Shh,' said Taro. 'I'm here now. Let's get you out.'"

---

## Shot 8 — BUILDING A LEVER (2:00–2:20)

**Visual Prompt:**
`A purple taro chunk carefully stacking loose floor tiles, stones, and broken pottery to build a lever fulcrum under a fallen wooden beam. A sturdy stick wedged into position. The tiny taro ball watches with wide hopeful eyes. Smart, focused storybook illustration with stormy purple background and warm focused light on the construction.`

**Narration:**
> "'What are you doing?' 'Building a lever. It's physics. If I put the fulcrum here and the effort there, even someone as small and old as me can move a beam ten times his weight. Tài bàng le, right?'"

---

## Shot 9 — THE BEAM ROLLS (2:20–2:35)

**Visual Prompt:**
`A purple taro chunk pushing with all his might against a stick lever, the heavy wooden beam tilting and rolling just enough for the tiny baby taro ball to squeeze free. She pops out like a cork from a bottle. Triumphant, intense storybook illustration with stormy violet and golden effort glow.`

**Narration:**
> "He pushed with everything he had — every ounce of starchy, purple, steamed-to-perfection strength — and the beam groaned, tilted, and rolled. She popped free like a cork from a bottle. 'Xièxie!' she cried, throwing her tiny arms around Taro's middle."

---

## Shot 10 — DOWN THE STEPS TOGETHER (2:35–2:50)

**Visual Prompt:**
`The chunky purple taro and the tiny baby taro ball descending the rain-slicked stone steps of Jiufen together, hand in hand, the storm beginning to clear with patches of star-filled sky breaking through. Red lanterns swaying gently now. Heartwarming storybook illustration with purple twilight and warm gold.`

**Narration:**
> "They made their way down together, the enormous old taro and the tiny young taro ball, step by step through the howling purple storm. Twice he slid down three steps. Each time, she helped him up. 'Jiāyóu, Uncle Taro!'"

---

## Shot 11 — THE WARM SOUP (2:50–3:10)

**Visual Prompt:**
`Two taro balls — one large and chunky, one tiny — floating contentedly in a steaming bowl of golden ginger sweet soup, eyes closed in peaceful smiles. Grandma Lin smiling above. A little girl with a spoon. Cozy, intimate storybook illustration with warm golden, purple, and amber tones.`

**Narration:**
> "Grandma Lin made soup. Hot, sweet, gingery soup, with fresh taro balls bobbing on the surface like happy purple planets. 'Chī bǎo le ma?' 'I believe,' said Taro, lowering himself into the warm broth, 'it is time for me to become exactly what I was always meant to be.'"

---

## Shot 12 — GOODNIGHT (3:10–3:25)

**Visual Prompt:**
`Jiufen at quiet late evening, the storm fully passed, red lanterns glowing softly along the stone steps, the ocean calm and silver in the moonlight. A bowl of warm taro soup sits on a windowsill. Sleepy, peaceful storybook illustration fading into purple and amber.`

**Narration:**
> "Tonight, little one, may you have the patience of Taro — the wisdom to wait, the strength to keep climbing, and the warmth to know that you'll always get there in the end. Storms pass. Steps lead somewhere. Goodnight, purple dreamer. Goodnight."

## Production Notes

- **Character consistency:** Taro = chunky purple oval, dusty purple skin with lavender-white swirls visible on cross-section, tiny round reading glasses (lost from Shot 6 onward), sturdy stubby legs, calm wise face. Tiny taro ball = pale purple sphere, barely an inch, huge teary eyes. Grandma Lin = ancient, kind, slow careful hands.
- **Art style consistency:** Include "whimsical storybook illustration, lavender-purple palette with warm gold accents" in every prompt. Storm intensity peaks Shots 4-9, calms by Shot 10.
- **Audio:** Taro's voice calm, measured, slightly old-fashioned. Tiny taro ball's voice high, frightened. Wind howls and rain hammers throughout the storm sequence.
- **Music:** Gentle traditional Taiwanese strings during the calm shots. Build to dramatic orchestral storm music for Shots 4-9. Resolve to warm lullaby for Shots 11-12.
- **Transitions:** Slow dissolves throughout, EXCEPT for the storm sequence where quick cuts heighten urgency. Hold long on Shot 12.
- **Each clip:** 5–15 seconds via Seedance 2.0. Shot 6 (climbing in storm) and Shot 9 (the lever push) deserve longer holds for the emotional climax.
